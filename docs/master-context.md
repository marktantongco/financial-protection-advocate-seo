## Core Buyer Pain Points (Research-Based)

### Pain Point #1: Premium Loading
> "My premium jumped 50% after I filed a claim"

**Context:** Pacific Cross and some other insurers practice premium loading after claims. This is a major buyer concern, especially for those switching from HMOs where this doesn't happen.

**Content Angle:** Address this head-on. Explain why it happens, which providers do it, and how to prepare for it.

---

### Pain Point #2: Pre-Existing Condition Exclusions
> "My pre-existing condition isn't covered — they just told me now"

**Context:** Many buyers don't understand waiting periods (typically 6–12 months) and disclosure requirements. Non-disclosure can lead to claim denial.

**Content Angle:** Educate on waiting periods, disclosure best practices, and which providers have more favorable pre-existing condition policies.

---

### Pain Point #3: HMO Age-Based Pricing
> "HMOs get expensive as I age — and I have to renew every year"

**Context:** HMOs are prepaid and renewed annually. Premiums increase with age. There's no guaranteed renewability like with insurance.

**Content Angle:** Position health insurance as the long-term alternative with guaranteed renewability.

---

### Pain Point #4: HMO vs Insurance Confusion
> "I don't know the difference between HMO and health insurance"

**Context:** Most Filipinos use these terms interchangeably. They don't understand the fundamental differences in coverage type, renewability, and claims processes.

**Content Angle:** Create clear comparison content. Use tables, infographics, and plain language.

---

### Pain Point #5: Network Accessibility
> "I can't find a doctor in their network near me"

**Context:** Provider networks vary wildly — from 1,600 doctors to 30,000+. Hospital accreditation matters for cashless transactions.

**Content Angle:** Always include network size comparisons. Address provincial vs Metro Manila accessibility.

---

### Pain Point #6: Claims Process Delays
> "The claims process is slow — I had to pay out of pocket first"

**Context:** Reimbursement claims can take weeks. Letter of Authorization (LOA) for cashless hospitalization has specific requirements.

**Content Angle:** Provide step-by-step claims guides. Compare cashless vs reimbursement timelines per provider.

---

### Pain Point #7: Employment Transition Gaps
> "I'm not employed anymore — I lost my company HMO"

**Context:** Corporate HMOs don't follow the employee when they leave. This creates immediate coverage gaps.

**Content Angle:** Target career shifters and freelancers with transition guides. Emphasize portability of personal insurance.

## Audience Lessons Already Learned

These are key educational points that should be reinforced throughout all content:

1. **HMO ≠ health insurance** — HMOs are prepaid, year-to-year. Insurance grows long-term.
2. **Cheapest plan ≠ best** — Limited coverage costs more during emergencies.
3. **Pre-existing conditions require disclosure** — Disclose upfront or lose the claim.
4. **Network size matters** — 1,600–30,000 doctors varies wildly per provider.
5. **Stacking HMO + health insurance = maximum protection** — The layered approach.
