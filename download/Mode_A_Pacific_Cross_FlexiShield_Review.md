## What is Pacific Cross FlexiShield?

## Common Concerns + Honest Answers

### Concern #1: "My premium jumped 50% after I filed a claim"

**The Reality:** Premium loading exists with Pacific Cross. After significant claims, your renewal premium may increase based on your claims history.

**What to Do:**
- Budget for potential increases when planning your coverage
- Compare with providers like Etiqa that have more stable premium structures
- Consider a higher deductible plan to reduce premium loading risk

### Concern #2: "My pre-existing condition wasn't covered"

**The Reality:** Pacific Cross has a 6-12 month waiting period for pre-existing conditions. If you don't disclose a condition and it's discovered during a claim, it will be denied.

**What to Do:**
- **Always disclose** your complete medical history during application
- Budget for the waiting period — you'll need PhilHealth or savings during this time
- Ask about waiting period waivers for certain conditions

### Concern #3: "The network is too small"

**The Reality:** Pacific Cross has ~1,600 accredited providers. This is smaller than MediCard or PhilCare, which have 10,000+.

**What to Do:**
- Check if your preferred hospitals are accredited before buying
- Consider the stacking strategy: PhilHealth + HMO + Pacific Cross for maximum coverage
- Ask your agent for the latest accredited hospital list

## FAQ

### Q: Is Pacific Cross a legitimate insurance provider?

**A:** Yes. Pacific Cross is registered with the Insurance Commission of the Philippines and has been operating since 1995. They are a legitimate non-life insurance provider for health coverage.

### Q: How much does Pacific Cross FlexiShield cost per month?

**A:** Monthly premiums range from approximately ₱1,500 for basic coverage to ₱10,000+ for premium plans. The exact cost depends on your age, health history, and chosen benefit limit.

### Q: Can I use Pacific Cross at any hospital?

**A:** Cashless hospitalization is available at accredited hospitals only. At non-accredited hospitals, you'll need to pay upfront and file for reimbursement.

### Q: What happens if I have a pre-existing condition?

**A:** Pre-existing conditions are covered after a 6-12 month waiting period. You must disclose all conditions during application. Non-disclosure can result in claim denial.

### Q: Will my premium increase after I file a claim?

**A:** Possibly. Pacific Cross may apply premium loading based on your claims history. This is standard practice for indemnity insurance, but it's important to budget for potential increases.

### Q: Can I stack Pacific Cross with PhilHealth and an HMO?

**A:** Yes. In fact, this is recommended. PhilHealth covers the base, HMO handles routine care, and Pacific Cross provides catastrophic coverage and portability.
